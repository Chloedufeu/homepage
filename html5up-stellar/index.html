Chloe Dufeu

Chloe Dufeu is a physics and mathematics double major at Bard College. She is passionate about astrophyics and has engaged in multiple research projects with esteemed professors. She has attended the Bard Summer Research Internship (2023), Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) at the University of Utah (2024), and continues to do research during the semester at Bard.

Research

X-ray Analysis of Seyfert Galaxies

Chloe collaborated with Dr. Qi Feng to perform X-ray analysis on NGC 1068 and NGC 4151 using data from the NuSTAR and Swift-XRT telescope. Both Seyfert galaxies have possible evidence for high energy neutrino emissions making them very interesting sources.

X-Ray Analysis of Filament F.0173-0.413

Chloe began her astrophysics research work with Dr. Shuo Zhang in the Fall semester of 2022. She analyzed data from the NuSTAR telescope observation of filament F.0173-0.413. She also completed a joint spectral analysis of the NuSTAR data combined with Chandra data that had been previously analyzed by researcher Daniel Wang.

Building a Spectra Network of Phosphine

Chloe began her ongoing research work with Dr. Clara Sousa-Silva in the Fall semester of 2023. She is working on improving the spectrum network of phosphine Clara worked on for her PhD thesis.
